VISION, MISSION STATEMENT, AND GOALS.

Our vision is to inspire young children to explore the area of science.

The purpose for this is to give students an opportunity to participate in fun activities that will encourage them to develop critical thinking, experimental, collaboration, and presentation skills, depending on the activity agreed upon.

These session will be simple activities based around the areas of science where students can collaborate with their peers while learning fun concepts about science in everyday life.Â 

The focus is to engage with young students from ages 8-10 and help them explore the very basic areas of science. Try to provoke thinking in these students about why things work the way they do, how is this done, and encourage them to take interest in the functions of mundane activities.

Not only will they be able to develop important skills that will be incredibly beneficial and crucial for later education, but they will also be enjoying themselves while learning which encourages the students to want to learn and maybe spark a passion for science.


EVENTS

Making the Rainbow (10/03/2020)

Today, our students explored concepts of acids and bases.

Here are some key learning objectives for this activity:

  • Understand the properties of different chemicals, how they can be neutrals, acids, or bases.
  • pH indicators and the pH scale can be used to determine whether a solution is acidic or basic and the.
  • When an acid reacts with a base, the process is called naturalization.

In this activity, we used Hydrochloric acid (HCl) as the acid, Sodium hydroxide (NaOH) as the base, and universal indicator.
The students started by adding some acid into a tube along with a few drops of universal indicator. Then very carefully, the base is added, all without mixing. The result is a rainbow in a tube, created by the different regions of pH.
